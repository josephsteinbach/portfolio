About-

My interest in graphic design can be traced back to the meaningful impact of many different forms of art. Growing up in Minneapolis I was exposed to many things that would become influential to my work today. I loved everything from skateboard graphics and logos, comics, and graffiti, to the abstract fine art in museums or books and magazines. All of these things inspired and gave me great a euphoric feeling from their freedom of expression.

I am a student majoring in Web Design at Minneapolis College. My creative credentials include volunteer and studio artist at The Soap Factory, independent curator, as well as freelance designer.

My goal is to become a front end web and graphic designer. I solve design problems with visually pleasing, simple, balanced layouts and a touch of my own personal style. I enjoy working with modern typography concepts and unique graphic elements to create a fresh, original approach to web and graphic design problems.

My experiences with all of these different forms of art has given me a natural eye for elegant, urban aesthetics and would make me a valuable asset to any graphic/web design organization.
